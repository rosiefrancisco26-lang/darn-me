# OFWs Deserve Better: A 14-Year Journey for Change

## An Open Letter to My Fellow Overseas Filipino Workers

---

## Who We Are

If you are Filipino, the word **OFW** brings hope. It is our way to survive, to provide, to dream for our families back home.

We are called **modern heroes**—and rightfully so. Together, we contribute **20% of the Philippines' GDP**. We are the backbone of our nation's economy. The government celebrates us. But do they truly protect us?

For **14 years**, I have been an OFW. I know this struggle. I know the sacrifice. And I know we deserve better.

---

## The Reality We Face

### The Numbers Don't Add Up

A 20,000 peso salary sounds good—until you realize it must feed a family as big as a small community. After deductions for benefits, taxes, and employer fees, what actually reaches your family?

Our in-laws, our employers, they see 20k. But they don't see:
- The cost of living abroad
- The distance from family
- The stress, the loneliness, the sacrifice

### The Gaps in Protection

Right now, there is **no life insurance guarantee** for OFWs. If an employer abuses us, exploits us, or causes us harm, there's no safety net. Our families are left vulnerable.

There is **no proper monitoring** of our wellbeing. OWWA exists, but how many OFWs actually receive adequate support and oversight?

There is **no mental health assessment** before we leave. Thousands of OFWs suffer from stress, depression, and burnout—all while isolated from family and support systems.

There is **no retirement security**. After years of sacrifice, many OFWs reach retirement age with little pension or savings to show for it.

---

## What Must Change: My Advocacy

### For the Government

I am appealing to the Philippine Government to take action:

1. **Mandatory Life Insurance for Every OFW**
   - Ensure every OFW has life insurance coverage
   - This protects employers from using abuse as a cost-cutting measure
   - This protects our families if tragedy strikes

2. **Require OFW Financial Contributions**
   - Every OFW must contribute to SSS and Pag-IBIG
   - Every OFW must have a mandatory 2,000 peso monthly savings fund
   - This builds a pension and retirement security

3. **Strengthen OWWA Monitoring**
   - OWWA must actively monitor every OFW in their designated location
   - Regular check-ins, not just paperwork
   - Real support when we need it

4. **Mental Health Screening**
   - Every OFW must undergo a neuro/psychological assessment before departure
   - Purpose: to identify stress risks and provide preventive support
   - To help OFWs understand the emotional cost of leaving home

5. **LGU Responsibility to Our Families**
   - Every district must know how many OFWs they have
   - Local Government Units must ensure families left behind receive support
   - Ensure remittances reach families and are used wisely

### For OFW Families at Home

The families we leave behind must also step up:

1. **Financial Education for Children**
   - Children must learn that 20k is NOT unlimited
   - They must understand the sacrifice behind every peso
   - They must learn to spend wisely and contribute, not depend

2. **Spouses Must Work**
   - The burden cannot rest on one OFW alone
   - Spouses must seek employment to support the family
   - This reduces pressure on OFWs and builds family resilience

3. **Protect Against Family Parasites**
   - Extended family members who only take, never give
   - Families must learn to say "no" to unreasonable demands
   - Our sacrifice should not enable dependency

---

## Why This Matters

After 14 years, I've learned that being an OFW is not just about sending money. It's about:

- **Dignity** – We deserve respect for our contribution
- **Safety** – We deserve protection from exploitation
- **Security** – We deserve a future after our years of service
- **Family** – Our loved ones deserve stability, not just survival

When an OFW retires, they should have:
- ✅ Money to support themselves
- ✅ A pension they can rely on
- ✅ Peace of mind
- ✅ The ability to enjoy life
- ✅ Freedom from financial pressure
- ✅ Respect from their nation
